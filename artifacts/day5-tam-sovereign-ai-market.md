# BOTTOMS-UP TAM SIZING MODEL
## Sovereign and On-Premises Enterprise AI Market
**Author:** Irtaqa Naveed, Senior AI Product Manager
**Date:** July 2026
**Method:** Bottoms-up unit economics, not top-down percentage of large market report

---

## WHY BOTTOMS-UP

Top-down TAM models say things like "the global AI market is $500 billion and we are targeting 1% of it." That is not a model. That is a wish.

Bottoms-up TAM starts with real units: how many organizations exist, how many will buy, what will they pay. Every number must be defensible from first principles.

---

## STEP 1: TOTAL ADDRESSABLE ORGANIZATIONS

These are organizations that genuinely cannot use cloud AI due to data residency, sovereignty, or compliance requirements.

| Segment | Reasoning | Estimate |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Defense and intelligence agencies | 195 countries, each with defense ministry and intelligence agencies at national level | 500 |
| Regulated banks | 30,000 regulated banking institutions globally, top 10% with AI ambition and strict compliance | 3,000 |
| Healthcare systems | Hospitals and health systems in regulated markets including EU, UK, US, GCC, and India under mandatory patient data compliance | 10,000 |
| Government agencies below national level | State governments, municipal authorities, public sector organizations with data sovereignty requirements | 50,000 |

**Total addressable organizations: 63,000**

---

## STEP 2: THREE-YEAR ADOPTION RATE

These organizations are conservative, non-flexible, and slow to adapt. Procurement cycles run 6 to 18 months. Security reviews require months of validation. Budget approvals require multiple executive layers. Internal resistance to change is structural.

**Estimated 3-year adoption rate: 10%**

This is optimistic given the institutional inertia but justified by three accelerating forces in 2026. First, the Fable 5 export control incident in June 2026 forced board-level conversations about API dependency risk at regulated enterprises globally. Second, EU AI Act high-risk obligations are now fully enforceable, creating mandatory compliance timelines that cannot be deferred. Third, sovereign AI has become a national priority in GCC, South Asia, and Southeast Asia markets with dedicated government funding.

**3-year addressable buyers: 63,000 x 10% = 6,300 organizations**

---

## STEP 3: AVERAGE CONTRACT VALUE

Sovereign AI deployments are not commodity purchases. They require custom implementation, security validation, and ongoing support. Pricing reflects that complexity.

| Tier | Description | % of Buyers | Annual Contract Value |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Top | National defense, central banks, large government ministries | 10% | $2,000,000 |
| Mid | Regional banks, large hospitals, state government agencies | 40% | $300,000 |
| Small | Municipal governments, smaller regulated enterprises | 50% | $80,000 |

**Blended average annual contract value:**
(10% x $2,000,000) + (40% x $300,000) + (50% x $80,000)
= $200,000 + $120,000 + $40,000
= **$360,000 per organization per year**

Rounded to $400,000 for conservative modeling including upsell and expansion revenue in Year 2 and 3.

---

## STEP 4: TAM CALCULATION

TAM = Total 3-year buyers x Average Annual Contract Value

TAM = 6,300 x $400,000

**TAM = $2.52 billion per year**

---

## MARKET CONTEXT

This $2.5 billion TAM is specifically the sovereign and on-premises enterprise AI segment. It does not include the broader cloud AI market.

This segment is growing faster than the cloud AI market in 2026 for three reasons. The Fable 5 export control crisis demonstrated that cloud API dependency is a geopolitical risk, not just a compliance preference. EU AI Act enforcement has created hard deadlines that procurement teams can no longer defer. And GCC sovereign AI programs including HUMAIN in Saudi Arabia and G42 in UAE are driving billions in national infrastructure investment that requires on-premises AI capability.

Most AI PMs and most AI companies are competing for the cloud AI market. The sovereign and on-premises segment is underserved, growing fast, and requires a specific background to credibly serve. That background is what I bring.

## ASSUMPTIONS AND SENSITIVITIES

| Assumption | Base Case | Bear Case | Bull Case |
|------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Total addressable organizations | 63,000 | 40,000 | 100,000 |
| 3-year adoption rate | 10% | 5% | 20% |
| Average contract value | $400,000 | $200,000 | $600,000 |
| TAM result | $2.52B | $400M | $12B |

Even in the bear case, a $400 million market with high barriers to entry and low competition is a compelling opportunity. In the bull case, accelerated by regulatory tailwinds and sovereign AI investment, this segment approaches $12 billion.
